How Memory Should Work

PMIS doesn't just store.
It learns what matters.

Most memory systems treat every piece of information equally. PMIS watches what actually works, and remembers the winners.

The Cycle
Four things happen, every single conversation.
1
Before you ask, it already knows.
The moment a conversation starts, PMIS pulls everything it knows about your topic. Not a keyword dump — it ranks by what actually worked before, surfaces proven patterns first, and deprioritises old ideas that never led anywhere.
2
Work happens, informed by memory.
The retrieved context shapes everything: which approach to try, which pitfalls to avoid, which decisions are already locked in. You're never starting from scratch — you're building on every past conversation.
3
Key learnings are extracted and structured.
After the work is done, PMIS doesn't save a transcript. It extracts the insights that matter — specific, reusable, weighted by importance. Similar knowledge merges automatically. No duplicates, no noise.
4
Outcomes teach the system what's real.
When you score a result, PMIS adjusts the weight of every piece of knowledge that contributed. High-scoring patterns rise. Low-scoring patterns fade. Over time, the system converges on what actually works — not what sounded good in theory.
The Difference
Most memory systems remember. PMIS understands.
Typical Memory Systems
Store everything, hope for the best.
Every fact gets the same priority. Retrieval is a keyword search. Old irrelevant memories compete equally with proven knowledge. There's no concept of "this worked" vs "this didn't". The 100th conversation is no smarter than the first.
PMIS
Learn from outcomes, serve what's proven.
Knowledge earns its place. Every insight starts unproven and graduates through evidence. Proven patterns surface first. Unproven ideas get a slight penalty. Failed approaches quietly fade. The system gets measurably better with every scored task.
Key insight

Other systems ask: "Does this keyword match?"
PMIS asks: "Did this knowledge lead to a good outcome last time?"

That single difference is why PMIS scores 100% on Temporal Reasoning where competitors plateau at 62-98%. It's not just remembering what you knew — it's remembering when it mattered and whether it worked.

The Real Advantage
What benchmarks don't measure.
Evidence Loop
Knowledge earns its weight.
Supermemory stores facts with fixed importance. PMIS starts with an estimate, then adjusts based on real outcomes. After 5 scored tasks, evidence accounts for 85% of an insight's weight. Bad advice sinks. Good advice rises. No other system does this.
Zero Dependencies
Runs anywhere. Owns your data.
Supermemory needs cloud infrastructure. Mastra needs vector databases. Zep needs a hosted service. PMIS is one Python file, one SQLite database. It runs on your laptop, offline, with no API keys, no subscriptions, no data leaving your machine.
Convergence
Decisions lock in over time.
In long projects, PMIS tracks which decisions are settled and which are still open. Session 50 knows not to re-debate what was decided in session 3. Competitors treat every conversation as a blank slate — the same discussions happen again and again.
Cross-Domain Transfer
Lessons from one project help another.
When two different projects share structural patterns, PMIS detects the overlap and offers insights from one when working on the other. Your cold outreach learnings can inform your proposal writing — automatically, at a discounted confidence level.
